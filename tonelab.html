How It Works

One plugin. Five lanes.
Infinite combinations.

Traditional FX chains apply effects to the full frequency range. ToneLab changes that. Each lane pairs an effect with its own EQ — so you decide which frequencies receive that effect and at what intensity.

01
Five Dedicated Lanes

Each lane has a pre-defined effect — Chorus, Distortion, Reverb, Delay, and Saturation. No menus, no routing. Each effect lives in its own lane, always active.

02
Shape the Range

Each lane has its own EQ. Use it to define exactly which frequencies that lane's effect acts on. Push reverb into the mids only. Saturate just the low end. Keep the highs clean.

03
Blend the Result

All five lanes mix back into a single output. The result is a layered, frequency-aware sound design: the precision of surgical EQ with the character of your chosen effects.

What's Inside

Everything ToneLab is built to do. No fluff, no guesswork.

Core Concept
5 Color-Coded Lanes

ToneLab's five lanes each carry a pre-defined effect: Chorus, Distortion, Reverb, Delay, and Saturation. Every lane is always present and independent. Adjust its EQ and mix level and let it run.

Frequency Control
Per-Lane EQ

Every lane carries an EQ that acts as a frequency gate for its effect. The effect only applies where the EQ lets it through — giving you surgical control over where your processing lives in the spectrum.

Signal Flow
Parallel Architecture

Lanes run in parallel, not in series. Your dry signal feeds all five lanes simultaneously. Each processes its own frequency-shaped version and blends back into the output, preserving the integrity of the original signal.

Platform
Built with JUCE

ToneLab is developed in JUCE, the industry-standard framework for professional audio plugins. Compatible with VST3 and AU, designed to run cleanly in any modern DAW workflow.
